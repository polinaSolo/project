Mysterious world of the moon

  1. Moon's Age

    The Moon is approximately 4.5 billion years old, just like the Earth. It is believed to have formed from the debris left behind after a giant impact between Earth and a Mars-sized object called Theia

  2. Moon's Size

    The Moon has a diameter of about 3,474 kilometers (2,159 miles), which is roughly one-fourth the size of Earth. It is the largest moon in relation to its parent planet in the solar system.

  3. Lunar Phases:

    The Moon goes through different phases as it orbits the Earth. These phases, such as the full moon, crescent moon, and half moon, occur because of the changing angles between the Sun, Moon, and Earth.

  4. Moon's Color:

    The Moon appears gray from Earth, but its actual color is a light brownish-gray. The lunar soil contains minerals like iron, calcium, and magnesium, which contribute to its color.

  5. Moon's Influence:

    The Moon's gravitational pull affects various phenomena on Earth, such as the ocean tides. It also has a subtle gravitational influence on the Earth's atmosphere and causes atmospheric tides.

  6. Moon's Surface Features:

    The Moon's surface is marked by various features, including impact craters, mountains, valleys, and rilles (channels carved by ancient lava flows). The largest known impact crater on the Moon is the South Pole-Aitken Basin, which is about 2,500 kilometers (1,550 miles) in diameter.
